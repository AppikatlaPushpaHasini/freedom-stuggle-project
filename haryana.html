Freedom Fighter of haryana

Rao Tula Ram was a valiant leader and freedom fighter who played a significant role in the Indian Rebellion of 1857 against British colonial rule. He is considered a heroic figure in Haryana and northern India for his bravery and leadership.

Background of Rao Tula Ram

Born: December 9, 1825 Place: Rampura village, Rewari (in present-day Haryana) Community: Yadav (Ahir) clan He belonged to a royal family and became the chief (Rao) of Rewari after his father’s death.

Struggle of Rao Tula Ram

Rao Tula Ram, an Ahir chieftain from Rewari, Haryana, played a pivotal role in the 1857 Revolt against the British. He led a rebellion in the Rewari region, mobilizing local support and forming a provisional government under the banner of Mughal Emperor Bahadur Shah Zafar. He raised an army, established a workshop for manufacturing arms, and actively participated in battles against the British, including the battle of Nasibpur. 
                Here's a more detailed look at his actions:
Taking Control of Rewari:
On May 17, 1857, Rao Tula Ram and his followers, including his cousin Rao Gopal Dev, seized control of Rewari, removing the local tehsildar and forming a local government. 
Raising an Army:
He successfully raised an army of around 5,000 soldiers, supported by a substantial financial donation from the local Ahir community. 
Establishing a Workshop:
To ensure self-sufficiency and reinforce his rebellion, Tula Ram established a workshop in the Rampura fort to manufacture guns and ammunition. 
Supplying Delhi Rebels:
He provided significant assistance to the rebels in Delhi, including sending Rs. 45,000 and supplies to General Bakht Khan ten days before the fall of Delhi. 
Military Leadership:
Rao Tula Ram led his forces in battles against the British, including the battle at Nasibpur, where his initial charge caused considerable casualties to the British forces.
Fleeing After Crushing of Rebellion: Following the British crackdown led by Brigadier General Showers and Lieutenant Colonel Gerrard, Tula Ram was forced to flee to Afghanistan, where he later died in 1863. Rao Tula Ram's legacy in Haryana is one of courage and resistance against the British, and he is remembered as a key figure in the 1857 Revolt. Born in 1803, Tula Ram Rao was originally from Rewari in the Gurugram district of Haryana. He was the Chieftain of Rewari and the successor to the Jagir of Tej Singh Rao. He played an instrumental role in organizing the Great Revolt of 1857. He gathered some troops and fought the British forces near the fort of Rampura. After a short while his troops fought a major battle at Nasibpur near Narnaul and he was on the verge of victory against the British forces of Colonel Gerrard. However, the Britishers received the reinforcement of Naga Sadhus of Galta (Jaipur) and the Sikh Army of Jind, Kapurthala, and Patila to their rescue. Tula Ram Rao ordered a retreat. In a meeting at Kalpi, the leaders of the Great Revolt deputed Tula Ram Rao to head a mission to seek foreign help for the Revolt. Tula Ram Rao along with Ram Pandit, Tara Singh, and Nathwa Ram Mali led a mission to Iran to negotiate with the Russian Ambassador in Tehran. They also met Amir Dost Mohammad Khan of Afghanistan in Kandahar and they also reached Kabul for further negotiation with the leaders of Afghanistan. However, Tula Ram Rao fell ill in Kabul and died on September 08, 1862. He received a state funeral by the Afghan Government and his body was cremated at Delhi Gate in Kabul.
