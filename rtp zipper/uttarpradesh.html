Freedom Fighter of Uttar pradesh

Mangal Pandey is considered the first freedom fighter of India who played a key role in igniting the Revolt of 1857, also known as the First War of Independence.

Background of Mangal Pandey

Full Name:Mangal Pandey Birth & Death: Born: July 19, 1827, in Nagwa village, Ballia district, Uttar Pradesh Died: April 8, 1857, hanged in Barrackpore, West Bengal

Struggle of Mangal Pandey

Mangal Pandey, born in Uttar Pradesh, was indeed a key figure in the 1857 Sepoy Mutiny, which is also known as the First War of Indian Independence. He was a sepoy in the 34th Bengal Native Infantry and his act of defiance at Barrackpore in March 1857, sparked the rebellion. .
Cause of Rebellion: The immediate cause of his rebellion was the introduction of new cartridges for rifles, which were greased with animal fat (cow and pig), which offended the religious sensibilities of both Hindus and Muslims, who were sepoys in the East India Company's army. The Incident: On March 29, 1857, Mangal Pandey refused to use the new cartridges and attacked his commanding officers, sparking the mutiny at Barrackpore, says Wikipedia. Execution: He was arrested and executed by a firing squad on April 8, 1857, by order of a court martial. Legacy: Mangal Pandey is remembered as a symbol of early Indian resistance against British rule, says Britannica and is considered a forerunner of the First War of Independence. A film based on the sequence of events that led up to the mutiny entitled Mangal Pandey: The Rising starring Indian actor, Aamir Khan along with Rani Mukerji, Amisha Patel and Toby Stephens, directed by Ketan Mehta was released on 12 August 2005. The life of Pandey was the subject of a stage play titled The Roti Rebellion, which was written and directed by Supriya Karunakaran. The play was organized by Sparsh, a theatre group, and presented in June 2005 at The Moving Theatre at Andhra Saraswat Parishad, Hyderabad, Andhra Pradesh. Samad Iqbal, a fictional descendant of Mangal Pandey, is a central character in Zadie Smith's debut novel White Teeth.
