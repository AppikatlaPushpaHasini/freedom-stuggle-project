Freedom Fighter of Bihar

Kunwar Singh was a prominent leader in the Indian Rebellion of 1857, also known as the First War of Indian Independence. Here's a concise overview of his life and role:

Background of Kunwar Singh

< Early Life: Born in 1777 in Jagdispur, in present-day Bhojpur district of Bihar. Belonged to a royal Rajput family (Ujjainiya clan). Became the Zamindar (landlord) of Jagdispur estate.

Struggle of Kunwar Singh

Kunwar Singh, a zamindar from Jagdishpur in Bihar, led the 1857 Revolt in Bihar against the British. He assumed command of the revolt at an advanced age, demonstrating strong leadership and tactical skills. His forces, composed primarily of local fighters, used guerrilla warfare to effectively harass British troops. They besieged the British settlement at Arrah, showcasing their ability to coordinate and sustain military operations. Kunwar Singh's leadership united various factions across different districts, expanding the revolt's impact.
Assumed Command: Kunwar Singh took charge of the sepoys at Danapur in July 1857. Siege of Arrah: He and his troops laid siege to the district headquarters at Arrah, holding the fort for several days. Guerrilla Warfare: Kunwar Singh was known for his expertise in guerrilla warfare, using hit-and-run tactics to harass the British. Liberated Jagdishpur: He liberated Jagdishpur and Arrah, demonstrating the capacity of local forces to challenge British control. Resilience: Despite British efforts to retake Jagdishpur, Kunwar Singh's forces successfully repelled attacks. Influence: His leadership united different factions across districts like Patna and Shahabad, and even influenced uprisings in areas like Chhotanagpur and Manbhum. Last Battle: He fought his last battle against Captain Le Grand near Jagdishpur in April 1858, suffering a severe repulse but remaining victorious.
Death On 23rd April, Singh defeated the force led by Captain le Grand and gained a victory near Jagdishpur. Veer Kunwar Singh dedicatedly fought and slew the British invaders despite being badly wounded. The British had to concede defeat ultimately. Instead of the Union Jack, the saffron flag of Jagdishpur was fluttering on top of the fort. Kunwar Singh was successful in liberating his motherland yet again. However, sadly, he did not live long to cherish his victory. He took his last breath on the 26th of April, 1858.Conclusion: Kunwar Singh’s contribution as a military commander in the Indian rebellion of 1857 against the British forces was truly an act of bravery. He fought them by hook or by crook until his last breath. There are very few warriors who fought a war, the same age as Kunwar Singh. He is truly an inspiration for the youth to have the courage and faith in themselves to combat the obstacles in life. 23rd of April in Bihar is marked as a public holiday as Veer Kunwar Singh Jayanti celebrates Kunwar Singh’s courage and contribution to his motherland during the Indian rebellion of 1857.
