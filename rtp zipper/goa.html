Freedom Fighter of Goa

Tristão de Bragança Cunha, commonly known as T. B. Cunha, was a prominent Goan freedom fighter, nationalist, and a pioneer of the anti-colonial movement against Portuguese rule in Goa. He is often honored as the "Father of Goan Nationalism" for his relentless efforts to integrate Goa with independent India and end centuries of Portuguese colonial rule.

Background of T. B. Cunha

Born: April 2, 1891, in Panjim, Goa (then under Portuguese rule) Died: September 26, 1958, in Bombay (Mumbai) Education: He pursued his higher education in France, where he studied engineering at the University of Paris (Sorbonne). While in Europe, he was deeply influenced by the ideas of liberty, democracy, and nationalism. His exposure to European political thought, especially the concepts of anti-colonial struggle, played a key role in shaping his political ideology.

Struggle of T. B. Cunha

T. B. Cunha, was a Portuguese nationalist and anti-colonial activist. Referred to as the "Father of Goan nationalism", he was the organiser of the first movement to end Portuguese rule in Goa.— Slogan by T. B. Cunha
