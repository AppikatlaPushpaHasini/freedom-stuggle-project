Freedom Fighter of Kerala

Velu Thampi Dalawa (also known as Velu Thampi or Velu Thampi Pillai) was a prominent freedom fighter and the Dalawa (Prime Minister) of the Kingdom of Travancore in the early 19th century. He is remembered for his resistance against the British colonial rule in India, particularly his leadership during the Velu Thampi Revolt (also called the Travancore Rebellion), which was a significant part of the larger Indian resistance to British expansion.

Background of Velu Thampi Dalawa

            Born: 1765
            Died: 1809
            Place of Birth: Mavelikkara, Travancore (Present-day Kerala)

Struggle of Velu Thampi Dalawa

In 1808, Velu Thampi Dalawa became increasingly disillusioned with the British interference in the internal matters of Travancore. The British sought to reduce the kingdom's autonomy, which was a violation of the agreements made with Travancore's rulers. Velu Thampi was deeply committed to preserving the sovereignty of Travancore and its people.

Velu Thampi organized raids on British outposts, utilized guerrilla tactics in the forests and rural areas, and sought to disrupt British supply lines. He also focused on raising awareness among the local population about the dangers of British rule and their oppressive policies.

Legacy

After the failure of the revolt, Velu Thampi Dalawa was captured by the British forces. However, rather than surrendering, he committed suicide in 1809 to avoid the disgrace of being captured and paraded as a prisoner.

Historical Significance: Velu Thampi Dalawa’s Revolt is considered a precursor to the Indian Rebellion of 1857. His actions inspired future generations of freedom fighters and helped build a strong sense of resistance against British rule in the southern regions of India.
