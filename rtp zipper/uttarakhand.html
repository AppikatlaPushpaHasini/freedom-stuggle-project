Freedom Fighter of uttarakhand

Veer Chandra Singh Garhwali was a courageous Indian soldier, freedom fighter, and social reformer, best remembered for his bold act of defiance against British orders during the Indian independence movement.

Background of Veer Chandra Singh

Born: December 25, 1891 Place: Garsain, Chamoli district, Garhwal (in present-day Uttarakhand)Came from a humble background in the Garhwal region of the Himalayas.

Struggle of Veer Chandra Singh

Veer Chandra Singh Garhwali, a hero from Uttarakhand, famously refused to fire on unarmed civilians in Peshawar during the British rule, according to a Facebook post. This act of defiance, which occurred during the 1930 Peshawar revolt, prevented a potential massacre similar to the Jallianwala Bagh incident.
The Incident: On April 23, 1930, thousands of Pathans were peacefully protesting in Peshawar. British authorities ordered the Garhwal Rifles, led by Chandra Singh Garhwali, to fire upon the protestors. Garhwali's Defiance: Chandra Singh, however, refused to obey the command, instructing his men to lower their rifles instead. He famously shouted, "Garhwali, cease fire!". The Aftermath: This act of courage and resistance led to Chandra Singh's imprisonment for 11 years, but it also became a significant event in India's freedom struggle. He is remembered for his bravery and for preventing a potential tragedy, according to the Ministry of Culture. July 1955, the UP government sent him an enthusiastic letter stating that it had increased his pension by a princely sum of 14 Rupees per month for his lifetime.”
(Excerpt from Sankrityayan’s biography of Chandra Singh Garhwali) It’s a matter of great shame that the government valued Garhwali’s contributions to the freedom struggle at no more than 14 rupees a month. This is but one example of how many freedom fighters were treated post independence. On October 1, 1971 Chandra Singh Garhwali passed away in penury. It is a sad irony that in this age of glitter and showmanship, Garhwali’s life and contributions are referenced by casteist politicians and campaigners in their speeches, without any understanding of the ideals and values he stood for. But we call on the people of India to remember justly this hero of the Peshawar revolt and a lifelong champion of communal harmony and solidarity.
