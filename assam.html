Freedom Fighter of Assam

Kanaklata Barua was an Indian freedom fighter who became a symbol of courage and sacrifice during the Indian independence movement. Born on December 22, 1924, in the village of Bismail in Assam, she was a young and spirited patriot who played a pivotal role in the struggle against British colonial rule.

Background of Kanaklata Barua

Kanaklata was born into a poor family in Assam. She lost her father at an early age, which left her family struggling. Despite this, she was raised with strong values of patriotism and love for her country. Her mother, in particular, played a significant role in instilling these values in her.

Struggle of Kanaklata Barua

Kanaklata Barua, a 17-year-old freedom fighter, was martyred during the Quit India Movement in Assam while leading a procession to hoist the national flag at Gohpur Police Station on September 20, 1942. She was a member of the "Mrityu Bahini" (Death Squad) and was shot dead by the police while holding the Tricolour.
Quit India Movement: The Quit India Movement was a mass civil disobedience campaign launched by Mahatma Gandhi in 1942 to demand immediate independence from British rule. Kanaklata Barua's Role: Kanaklata Barua was a key figure in the Quit India Movement in Assam, particularly in the Gohpur region. She was part of the "Mrityu Bahini," a youth group formed to actively participate in the movement. The Gohpur Incident: On September 20, 1942, Kanaklata Barua led a procession of unarmed villagers towards the Gohpur Police Station to hoist the Indian national flag.
Martyrdom: As the procession approached the police station, the police opened fire, and Kanaklata Barua was shot dead. She was holding the Tricolour at the time of her death, symbolizing her courage and sacrifice for the country. Impact: Kanaklata Barua's martyrdom became a symbol of resistance against British rule and inspired other freedom fighters. She is remembered as a brave young woman who sacrificed her life for India's independence. During the Quit India Movement Barua joined the Mrityu Bahini, a death squad comprising groups of youth from the Gohpur sub-division of Assam. On 20 September 1942, the Bahini decided she would hoist the nationalist flag at the local police station. Barua led a procession of unarmed villagers to do so. The police under Rebati Mahan Som, the officer in charge of the police station, warned the procession of dire consequences if they proceeded with their plan. Even after the warning, the procession continued marching ahead when the police fired upon the procession. Barua was shot and the flag she was carrying with her was taken up by Mukunda Kakoti who too was shot at. Both Barua and Kakoti were killed in the police action. Barua was 17 years old at the time of her death
